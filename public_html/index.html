3. Some universities pretend rape doesn't exist.

Source: PublicIntegrity.org: Sexual Assault on Campus

Few, if any of perpetrators face punishment from a school.

Source: PublicIntegrity.org: A lack of consequences for sexual assault

Many colleges work against victims by trying to cover up sexual assault cases.

Under Title IX, when a sexual assault has occurred, universities must:

1. diffuse the “hostile environment”
2. prevent the future occurrence of sexual assaults
3. restore victims’ lives

However, few schools comply.

Source: The Center for Public Integrity: A lack of consequences for sexual assault

How universities are treating victims:

"I asked an administrator what the process [of reporting a rape] would look like. Instead, that person told me, 'Rape is like a football game, Annie. If you look back on the game, and you’re the quarterback and you’re in charge, is there anything that you would have done differently in that situation?’”

- Annie Clark, UNC Chapel-Hill graduate

"I was so distraught and so alone I was in more pain worrying about talking about it than just going forward. I feared the shame of telling administrators that I needed help."
"These are felonies that are basically being treated as a slap on the wrist."

- Andrea Pino, UNC Chapel-Hill

[On school officials' delay in filing her report]: "Apparently some very serious things had happened that week. And I felt like screaming, 'Well hello, I was raped. Is that not serious enough for you?'”

- Samantha, College of William and Mary

(On the administration): "It's incredibly clear that those people had no idea what sexual assault is, what consent is."
“It has become clear that this case was no longer about me. It was all about politics, power and compliance.”


- Landen Gambill, UNC Chapel-Hill

"A college or university often times is very afraid of what will happen to their enrollment, questions from parents: questions from contributors. It looks bad for the college."

- Daniel Carter, vice president of victims rights group Security on Campus

(On the rape stastistics of college women): "One in five. That number applied to anything else would be viewed as an epidemic.”

- Carol McCord, associate dean of students of Indiana University
